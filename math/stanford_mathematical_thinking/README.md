## Notes

### Mathematical Thinking, Language, Proof, Numbers

One compact way to relate the course themes:

* **Mathematical thinking**: The shift from executing procedures to reasoning about ideas.
* **Precise language**: The habit of making claims clear enough to analyze.
* **Proof**: The structured justification that shows why a claim follows.
* **Numbers**: A familiar domain where proof techniques become concrete.

In other words: mathematical thinking starts with precise language, turns claims into proof obligations, and uses examples such as numbers to practice rigorous reasoning.

#### Learning example

* **Vague claim**: “This pattern always works.”
* **Precise claim**: State the domain, assumptions, and exact conclusion.
* **Proof task**: Show the conclusion follows for every permitted case.
* **Number example**: Prove divisibility, parity, or inequality claims from definitions.

If a proof feels convincing but has a hidden assumption, the issue usually lies in imprecise language or an unjustified inference.

#### Conceptual chain

You can summarize the course arc like this:

> Mathematical thinking → precise language → proof → reasoning about numbers

The course is about learning to think like a mathematician rather than only calculating like one.
